About Me

Minatozaki Sana is a Japanese singer, dancer, and member of the internationally popular K-pop girl group TWICE, formed by JYP Entertainment. Born on December 29, 1996, in Osaka, Japan, Sana is celebrated for her vibrant personality, infectious smile, and impressive stage presence.

She moved to South Korea as a teenager to pursue her dream of becoming an idol and debuted with TWICE in 2015 after competing on the survival show SIXTEEN. Known for her charming catchphrase “Shy Shy Shy” from the hit song Cheer Up, Sana quickly captured the hearts of fans worldwide.

Sana’s vocal abilities and graceful dance skills complement her warm, approachable demeanor, making her a fan favorite. Beyond music, she is also recognized for her stylish fashion sense, cheerful energy, and positive influence in the entertainment industry. Her journey from Japan to becoming a global star serves as an inspiration to aspiring artists and fans alike.
